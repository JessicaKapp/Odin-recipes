Katsudon

A Delicious Dish

This succulent pork and rice dish will have you constantly coming back for more! One would think that, as a traditional japanese dish, this would be difficult to recreate in the american kitchen. Thankfully that isn't the case!

Katsudon can be easily replicated with things you can find at any american grocery store. Some traditional items, like dashi, have been subbed out for more convenient items but over all the taste is still very good. Please give it a try!

Ingredients

  • Two boneless, skinless pork chops
  • Three eggs, one for egg wash and two set aside
  • All purpose flour
  • Panko bread crumbs
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup white rice
  • 1/2 of a large white onion (green onion is also acceptable)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp mirin

Preparation

  1. Start by taking the pork chops and pounding them out to about a 2 cm thickness.
  2. Lightly coat the thin chops in all purpose flour, dip in egg, and then coating liberally with panko bread crumbs. make sure to season the bread crumbs to taste.
  3. Pour 1/4 cup of vegetable oil into a large frying pan and bring to medium heat. Add both chops and pan fry for 3-5 min on each side. Allow to drain on a papertowel when finished. While the pork chops are cooling use this time to dice your white onion and beat two eggs until mixed.
  4. Drain the excess oil and add your onions to the pan. Saut√© until translucent.
  5. While the onion is cooking add together the soy sauce, chicken broth, sugar, and mirin. Then add in your soy sauce and broth mixture to the pan. Stir for a few seconds in order to remove the frond from the bottom of them pan.
  6. Add your pork and eggs back into the pan. Cook on medium low heat until egg has just set. Once the egg is finished serve on top cooked white rice.
  7. Enjoy!
